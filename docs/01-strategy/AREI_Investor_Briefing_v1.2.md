**Eyebrow:** 01 · MARKET POSITION

**Headline:**
African property markets are digitally fragmented. The data layer is missing.

**Body:**
Listings are scattered across hundreds of broker sites, portals, and informal channels. Asking prices, square meters, and location fields are inconsistent. No African property market has a shared benchmark.

International capital, diaspora investors, and institutional buyers are already moving in. The infrastructure to read the market is not.

**SIGNAL**
No published asking-price index. No comparable benchmark across markets.

**OUTCOME**
Capital is cautious. Transactions are inefficient. Pricing is guesswork.

**Eyebrow:** 03 · DEFINITION

**Headline:**

**Body:**

**Three-layer block (matches brand "Records · Methodology · Signal"):**

Public listings, broker sites, portal feeds, source registries — the raw market footprint.

**L2 · METHODOLOGY**
Ingest, normalize, deduplicate, score. Scoring rules, sample frame, index formula.

**L3 · SIGNAL**
The published index value. Country-level listing-based market signal, updated monthly. Methodology is public.

**Bridge line:**
One pipeline. Three surfaces built on top of it.

## SLIDE 08 — THE DATA ENGINE

**Eyebrow:** 07 · ENGINE

**Headline:**
The asset is not the website. The asset is the cleaned, scored, historical dataset.

**Body:**
A six-stage pipeline turns raw listings into indexed, scored records. Each listing becomes a record: normalized price, square meters, location, broker, source, image state, completeness score, duplicate risk. Records that pass go into the index.

**Six-stage horizontal flow:**

SOURCES → INGEST → NORMALIZE → DEDUPLICATE → SCORE → PUBLISH

**Sub-row of detail:**
- **Sources:** 17 mapped in Cape Verde, registry expandable per market
- **Ingest:** scheduled crawl, structured feed where available
- **Normalize:** AI-assisted field extraction (square meters, location, property type)
- **Deduplicate:** cross-source matching, duplicate-risk score
- **Score:** completeness, freshness, image coverage, source reliability
- **Publish:** records that pass the methodology threshold flow to the public index

**Body footer:**
Engine improvements compound. Each new market makes the parser more general. Each month of operation adds historical depth that competitors cannot replicate without time.

**Disclosure line:**
Current engine version: AREI methodology v0.3. Engineering roadmap in appendix.

## SLIDE 11 — COMPETITION & DEFENSIBILITY

**Eyebrow:** 10 · POSITION

**Headline:**
No incumbent has the incentive to build this. That is the opening.

**Body:**
The competitive landscape divides into three groups. None of them are building what AREI is building.

**Comparison block:**

**Consumer portals — Property24, Lamudi, local broker sites**
Optimize for listings volume and consumer leads. Do not publish methodology, deduplicate across sources, or maintain comparable historical series. AREI distributes their listings to international buyers — not a direct competitor.

**International data publishers — CoStar, Knight Frank Data, JLL Research**
Cover mature commercial markets and prime residential in OECD cities. Do not cover fragmented African residential markets at the country level. Coverage gap, not direct overlap. Potential data partner long-term.

**Local brokerage research notes**
Optimize for internal sales and anecdotal commentary. Do not publish comparable, sourced, cross-period series. Replaced as the reference source once AREI is published.

**Defensibility block:**

**Cumulative dataset.** Every month of operation adds historical depth a new entrant cannot replicate without time.

**Source graph.** Broker relationships, source reliability scores, and ingestion infrastructure compound across markets.

**Methodology authority.** The published methodology becomes the citation standard. Hard to dislodge once established.

**Brand architecture.** The "Country Real Estate Index" naming structure is repeatable, institutional, and SEO-defensible across markets.

## SLIDE 14 — RISKS

**Eyebrow:** 13 · RISKS

**Headline:**
The risks we see, and how we are managing them.

**Body:**
Three risks are material. Each has a defined response.

**RISK 01 · Transaction price data is not publicly accessible in target markets.**

*Why it matters.* A transaction-price index is the strongest possible market signal.

*Response, near-term.* AREI is positioned from day one as a listing-based market signal, not a transaction index. The methodology, the brand, and the disclosure line on every published value make this explicit. The product is defensible without closing prices.

*Response, long-term.* Closing-price data becomes accessible through institutional partnerships — national property registers, ministries of housing, central banks, and investment promotion agencies. AREI's editorial and methodology-first positioning is designed to make these partnerships possible. A listing-based index published with credibility is the precondition for being trusted with closing-price data. Without that step, no agency engages.

**RISK 02 · Brokers resist data collection.**

*Why it matters.* Broker cooperation improves data quality and reduces the cost of maintaining parsers.

*Response.* Broker Tools v1 is free and gives brokers international distribution they cannot build themselves. The relationship is structured as exchange, not extraction. Public listings remain accessible under standard practice in parallel.

**RISK 03 · Cape Verde proves too small to justify infrastructure cost.**

*Why it matters.* If the playbook does not generalize, the investment thesis breaks.

*Response.* The architecture is designed for repeatability from the first line of code. Cape Verde is the test market, not the destination. Ghana onboarding begins at month 9. If parser generalization fails, the thesis fails — and we will know within 18 months, before significant institutional capital is at risk.
