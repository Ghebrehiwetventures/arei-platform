## 1. Purpose

This is the working playbook for first broker conversations in Cape Verde. One document. Use it before every meeting, capture notes after.

---

## 2. Strategic frame

- AREI is not a broker and not replacing agencies
- AREI indexes public listing data and structures it into a market signal
- Cape Verde is Market 01 — the proof market, not the destination
- Brokers get attribution, international visibility, and buyer intent
- AREI gets better data quality and source cooperation
- The relationship is exchange, not extraction

---

## 3. First meeting support sheet

**Goal**
Research call. Understand how international buyers behave. Test whether the AREI model makes sense to the broker. Not a sales call.

**Recording**
Record on iPhone. Transcribe after. Upload to ChatGPT. Update broker strategy based on real feedback.

**Opening**
- I'm building Africa Real Estate Index, starting with Cape Verde
- The goal is to make the market easier for international buyers to understand
- We index public listing data and point buyers back to the original agency
- We're not a competing agency
- Their listings are probably already on the site — treat this as normal, not a problem
- "I want to make sure it is useful for you and accurate for buyers"
- Stop. Listen.

**If listings come up**
- Calm and direct: we index public data, we point buyers back to the original agency
- If something is wrong or they want changes, we fix it
- This is normal index coverage, not a crisis

**Buyer lead questions**
- How do your international buyer leads come in today?
- Which channels bring the most serious buyers?
- Where do deals usually fall through?

**International buyer problems**
- What do international buyers usually misunderstand about Cape Verde?
- What questions do they ask again and again?

**Market visibility**
- Is Cape Verde easy to compare online for someone researching from abroad?
- What is missing in how properties are presented online?

**Agency perspective**
- What part of dealing with international buyers takes the most time?
- What still feels inefficient?

**Broker tools direction**
- If the index is useful, the next step is simple broker tools: better listing structure, direct buyer intent, correction control, lightweight lead handling
- Not a CRM
- Only mention if they ask what comes next

**Trust and control**
- Would a neutral index that sends buyer attention back to the original agency be useful or just noise?
- What would feel risky about being included?
- What would need to be true for them to feel comfortable?

**Close**
- This is very helpful — we're still early and mainly trying to understand the agency side
- Would they be open to giving feedback as we develop the first version?
- If we run a small pilot later, would it be okay to show it to them?

**After-call notes**
- Lead sources
- Buyer pain points
- Broker concerns and objections
- Pilot interest
- Listing or data concerns
- Next step

## 4. CCore notes

- CCore has been contacted
- Their listings may be on the site — treat this as normal index coverage, not a crisis
- Use confident transparency language: "Your listings are already on the site. We index public data and point buyers back to you."
- Do not apologise for indexing public data
- Ask for feedback on usefulness and accuracy

---

## 5. What not to say

- "We scraped your listings" → use "we index public listing data"
- "We already listed your properties" as a defensive or apologetic phrase
- Guaranteed leads — the lead inbox is in development, not promised
- Complete market coverage — 17 sources mapped, not complete
- Marketplace language — AREI is an index, not a marketplace
- CRM promises — Broker Tools v1 is not a CRM

---

## 6. Pilot logic

A pilot is low-commitment. What it includes:

- **Source attribution** — agency name and contact on every listing attributed to them
- **Direct contact path** — inquiries go straight to the broker, not through AREI
- **Correction and removal option** — if something is wrong, they flag it and AREI fixes it
- **No lock-in** — they can ask for removal at any time
- **Optional source cooperation** — direct feed is the next step, not the first ask

---

## 7. Open decisions

- **Removal and correction policy** — turnaround time needs to be a real number before it is promised in a meeting
- **Broker tools readiness** — when are tools ready to mention as a product, not just a direction?
- **Which broker conversations happen next** — prioritise warm intros over cold outreach; who do we know in the Cape Verde broker market?
