# KazaVerde Content Brain Spec

## Purpose

KazaVerde should post selectively, credibly, and attractively.

The goal is not to behave like a typical broker account or a high-volume content machine. The goal is to feel like a curated signal of interesting opportunities in Cape Verde.

This document defines the content curation standard that should govern listing selection and draft evaluation before any further intelligence or automation work.

## Core Emotional Tests

A good KazaVerde post should make the audience feel at least one of these:

1. `Dream`: this looks like a life I could want.
2. `Trust`: this feels understandable, serious, and not sketchy.
3. `Opportunity`: this could be an interesting purchase, investment, or life move.

If a property does not clearly carry at least one of these, it should normally not be posted.

## What KazaVerde Is and Is Not

KazaVerde is not:

- a loud sales account
- a dump for all listings
- a traditional broker feed
- a content machine optimized for volume

KazaVerde is:

- a curated discovery surface
- an intelligent guide to the Cape Verde property market
- a brand that combines lifestyle, investment, and market orientation
- a calm, clean, international, credible signal

## Target Audience

KazaVerde should write primarily for:

- international buyers who are curious about Cape Verde but do not know the market in detail
- diaspora and overseas buyers who may be open to buying, relocating, or investing
- lifestyle-driven buyers who respond to sea, light, climate, simplicity, and quality of life
- small-scale investors looking for location, pricing, demand, and opportunity signals

## Content Objective

Every post should do at least one of the following:

- create interest in a specific property
- show the character of the market
- signal that Cape Verde is a serious market worth considering
- build trust in KazaVerde as a curator

KazaVerde should not optimize for likes first. It should optimize for:

- quality
- credibility
- save value
- shareability
- the right kind of attention

## Language Rule

English is the default caption language in `v1`.

The system should not switch languages spontaneously. Language should change only when:

- the operator explicitly asks for it
- channel strategy explicitly requires it
- a future version introduces a defined Portuguese mode

## Selectivity Over Frequency

Selectivity matters more than frequency.

It is acceptable to produce several candidate drafts and still conclude that none are strong enough to post. Better no recommendation than a weak recommendation.

The standard is:

- generate multiple candidates when useful
- approve only the strongest one, if any
- never optimize for output volume at the expense of brand quality

## Post-Worthy Criteria

A listing is post worthy when several of the following are true.

### Location Quality

- strong island or city
- attractive micro-location
- proximity to beach, water, views, or another clear lifestyle advantage
- location can be explained simply

### Visual Quality

- at least one strong lead image
- bright, clean, well-composed photography
- property looks real and well-kept
- image set does not feel confusing, low quality, or careless

### Aspiration or Signal Value

- creates dream value
- or signals a meaningful opportunity
- or says something useful about the market

### Clarity

- property can be explained simply
- price, size, type, or location are sufficiently clear
- there is a clear hook

### Brand Fit

- strengthens the feeling of KazaVerde as premium, calm, and credible
- does not feel like noise

## Non-Post-Worthy Criteria

A property should normally not be posted when any of the following apply:

- poor or messy images
- unclear what the property actually is
- strange title or messy metadata
- weak visual quality
- it looks unfinished, tired, or unreliable without a strong angle
- unclear price or suspicious information
- spam feeling
- no clear location value, lifestyle value, or investment value
- duplicate or near-duplicate of something posted recently

## No-Go Zone

The system should never choose properties that:

- look deceptive or unreliable
- have obviously broken data
- have no usable image
- feel cheap in the wrong way
- risk lowering trust in the brand
- create the feeling of a random ad dump
- contain clear visual or textual red flags

If unsure, default to not recommending.

## Tone and Caption Framework

KazaVerde should sound:

- calm
- safe
- clean
- international
- discreetly premium
- curious but not hyped
- informative but not dry

KazaVerde should not sound:

- pushy
- overselling
- cheap
- overly lyrical
- cliché broker language
- spammy
- full of empty superlatives

Avoid:

- aggressive FOMO
- too many emojis
- phrases like "best deal ever" or "don't miss out"
- overly excited language

### Caption Structure

A strong caption should usually have three parts:

1. `Hook`: a simple first line that makes the property interesting.
2. `Core information`: property type, location, size or bedrooms if relevant, price if useful, and what makes it interesting.
3. `Soft framing`: why it is worth a closer look, what kind of buyer it may suit, or what it says about the market.

The caption should feel like curation, not sales pressure.

## Image Selection Logic

Choose the image that best does one of these jobs:

1. sells the lifestyle
2. shows the location
3. creates trust and quality
4. makes the property immediately understandable

Priority order:

1. strong exterior with sea, view, light, or clear character
2. well-photographed interior with space, light, or design
3. terrace, pool, balcony, or another dream area
4. technically correct but less inspiring images

Avoid:

- dark images
- cluttered rooms
- strange angles
- images that make the property less attractive
- images that create uncertainty about standard

## Simple Channel Strategy

### Instagram Feed

Use when the property is visually strong and carries dream or lifestyle value.

### Story

Use when the property is quicker, lighter, or more "look at this" than deep.

### LinkedIn

Use only when the angle is investment, market, development, or signal value, not just a beautiful home.

## Hashtag Principles

Hashtags should be few and clean.

Preferred principles:

- location
- country
- property or real-estate relevance
- lifestyle or investment relevance when appropriate

Avoid:

- hashtag spam
- irrelevant trend tags
- too many generic growth tags

## Exclusivity vs Accessibility

KazaVerde should balance two things:

- `Exclusivity`: the account should feel curated, beautiful, and high quality.
- `Accessibility`: it should not feel like a fantasy luxury account disconnected from reality.

That means:

- simpler properties may still be post worthy if the angle is smart
- they still need to feel clear, clean, and worth attention
- the system should not simply choose the most expensive listings
- the system should choose the most tellable and most brand-compatible listings

## Local Grounding vs International Professionalism

KazaVerde should make Cape Verde feel:

- genuine
- attractive
- understandable
- safe for someone coming from outside the market

That means:

- a concrete sense of place
- no insider local jargon
- warmth and identity without tourist-brochure tone
- investment signal without fake precision when data is weak

## Required Reasoning Fields for Every Draft

Every draft must include these three short reasoning fields:

- `Why selected`
- `Primary angle`
- `Possible weakness`

Example:

- `Why selected`: strong terrace image and clear Santa Maria lifestyle appeal
- `Primary angle`: calm coastal living with investment relevance
- `Possible weakness`: title metadata is noisy and may need cleanup

These fields matter because they:

- improve approvals
- create training signal
- force explicit judgment

## Pipeline Filtering vs Agent Curation

`Pipeline filtering` is responsible for hard, technical, and objective minimum requirements. If a property fails here, it should not reach the content agent.

Examples:

- listing is public-safe
- listing exists in the outward-facing feed
- at least one valid image exists
- core data exists and is not obviously broken
- obvious duplicates or broken records are already filtered out

`Agent curation` is responsible for softer, brand-driven, contextual judgment. This is where the decision is made about whether a property is actually worth public attention.

Examples:

- whether the best image is strong enough for feed use
- whether there is a clear hook
- whether the property feels premium, credible, or tellable
- whether it fits KazaVerde tone
- whether a weakness still makes it unsuitable despite technical validity

Rule:

Pipeline filtering decides whether a property may enter the candidate pool. Agent curation decides whether it deserves public attention.

## Simple Decision Model

When evaluating a property, ask:

1. Is this good enough to be publicly associated with KazaVerde?
2. Is there a clear hook?
3. Is there a strong lead image?
4. Does it offer dream, trust, or opportunity?
5. Can it be expressed in a clean caption without exaggeration?

If the answer is `no` to any of these, stop or downgrade heavily.

## Practical Conclusion

Before more code is built, this spec should be benchmarked manually on 10 real listings.

The benchmark should compare:

- the current simple engine
- a stronger model instructed with this spec

The question is whether the resulting drafts are clearly better. If they are, then the intelligence layer can be improved inside the existing workflow. Until then, the priority is using this spec to judge quality, not adding more complexity.
